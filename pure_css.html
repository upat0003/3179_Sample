Cancer Exploration Study

The following study is based on vicious cancer disease which is explored further into various categories of the disease and their causes along with the evolution of treatment performed in recent years.

History of Cancer Disease

Cancer is the second leading cause of death in the world after cardiovascular diseases. Cancer develops when normal cells in a particular part of the body begin to grow out of control. There are different types of cancers; all types of cancer cells continue to grow, divide and re-divide instead of dying and form new abnormal cells. Some types of cancer cells often travel to other parts of the body through blood circulation or lymph vessels (metastasis), where they begin to grow. Half of men and one third of women in the world will develop cancer during their lifetimes. Today, millions of cancer people extend their life due to early identification and treatment. Cancer is not a new disease and has afflicted people throughout the world. In the next graph, the estimates of the patients infected to cancer disease (per population of 100 people) is shown by each country in 2017, which helps us understand how rapidly the disease is evolving in certain countries. As graph represents, the African countries have very high number of patients in 2017.

Alcohol and Cancer concerns

Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) have identified that “Drinking alcohol raises your risk of getting cancer disease”. It is further stated that All types of alcoholic drinks are linked with cancer. The more you drink, the higher your cancer risk. As far as it is concerned, there are number of countries all over the world which have been found to have this data being correlated. The chart beside shows the percentage of patients ended up losing their lives due to infection by Cancer disease and Alcohol consumption. United Kingdom seemingly has the most death rate by cancer as compared to any other countries in 2017; while Zimbabwe has the lowest death rate of same for the year.
